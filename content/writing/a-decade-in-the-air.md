---
title: "A Decade in the Air"
date: "2025-11-14"
excerpt: "Notes on what changes — and what does not — after ten years of aerial practice."
---

Year one is about hands. Year three is about fear. Year ten, it turns out, is about breath again, which is where I started, on a yoga mat, long before I touched fabric.

The strange gift of a long practice is that the apparatus stops being an obstacle and becomes a collaborator. The silk has opinions. The hoop has a temperament. You stop performing *on* them and start performing *with* them.

I founded Ozark Cirque in the middle of that shift, partly because I wanted other artists in the room — people whose collaborations with the apparatus looked nothing like mine.
