The first time a student showed me a wrap I did not know, I understood my thesis better than any literature review had managed.

Circus pedagogy inherited a master-apprentice shape from its family-troupe history, but the studio I want to run looks more like a lab. Two bodies, one apparatus, a shared problem: how does *this* body get *there*? The teacher holds safety and vocabulary; the discovery is joint.

> Co-learning is not the absence of expertise. It is expertise that stays curious.

This is also why I think circus belongs in social-emotional learning work. The apparatus is honest — it gives feedback without judgment — and progress is legible to the student before it is legible to anyone else. That is a rare and useful thing for a young person who has stopped trusting adult evaluations.
